How It Works

Preparation and refinement. From lived experience to living expertise.

LELI is a replicable framework for institutionalizing meaningful engagement of subject matter experts with lived experience. Here's the methodology — what consultants learn, how they're deployed, and why the model is built for county scale.

The Core Insight

Expertise delivers when it's prepared to.

The people who have navigated your county's systems hold knowledge no survey, dataset, or professional training can replicate: what services feel like from the inside, which handoffs hold, and where a small change would make an outsized difference.

Preparation is what makes that knowledge fully actionable.

🏛️

Knows which body

A consultant who understands your governance structure knows which body to bring a finding to — and when in the cycle it can still shape the decision.

💡

Knows what's achievable

A consultant fluent in your funding streams knows which recommendations are achievable within existing dollars — and which need a different vehicle.

🔗

Connects experience to action

A consultant who can read a strategic plan and interpret performance data connects what the community is experiencing to what the county can act on.

That's the refinement LELI institutionalizes. The experience stays the foundation. The preparation makes it deployable.

Built for County Scale

Counties are where the systems live.

The systems are county systems

Coordinated entry, homelessness response governance, behavioral health, public health, aging services, workforce boards — the infrastructure people with lived experience have navigated is largely administered at county scale.

One expert, many departments

Because lived experience spans multiple systems, one prepared consultant can advise across several county departments. Counties can co-fund a shared roster and multiply the return on a single preparation investment.

One program, every community

A county implementation serves every city and unincorporated area within it — one standing program instead of a patchwork of local efforts.

Cities — particularly large cities with their own service systems — can adapt the framework as well. Every implementation is scoped to the structure it serves.

The Framework · Step 01

Recruit.

Consultants are recruited from the communities the county's plans are written for: people with current or former lived experience of homelessness, who have navigated the county's response systems firsthand — and usually several neighboring systems as well.

Recruitment is community-rooted and designed to reach the experts counties most want to hear from: people living unsheltered, families in vehicles, youth on their own, veterans, elders, returning citizens, and people navigating homelessness alongside disability, mental health, or recovery. No policy background is required. That's what the preparation builds.

The Framework · Step 02

Prepare. Phase One · Months 1–4

Structured preparation across five domains:

County Structures & Governance

How the county actually works: which departments deliver which services, how governance bodies relate, how money flows in and through, and how decisions move from proposal to policy.

System Fluency

Deep working knowledge of the systems consultants will advise on — coordinated entry, data and performance infrastructure, continuum of care governance, funding mechanisms, eligibility structures — plus the neighboring systems each consultant has navigated.

Policy Language & Methodology

How to read a strategic plan. How to interpret system performance data. How to translate firsthand knowledge into findings that are documented, specific, and mapped to decisions a department can actually make.

Consulting & Research Skills

Running community feedback sessions, verifying patterns, documenting reasoning, and delivering findings in the formats decision-makers use — written submissions, testimony, briefings.

Directing AI as a Tool

Consultants learn to direct AI to handle scale — reading thousands of community inputs, translating across languages, flagging patterns — while judgment, interpretation, and meaning-making stay in human hands.

Phase One also includes structured introductions to county staff and system partners, so the working relationships consulting depends on are in place before deployment begins.

The Framework · Step 03

Deploy. Phase Two · Months 5–12

Consultants move into active advisory work across the county's real processes:

Plan Consultation

Reviewing drafts and delivering formal input while plans are being written.

Implementation Workgroups

Advising on program design as participants, helping implementation stay true to each plan's intent.

Evaluation & Performance Review

Reading system performance data alongside community feedback to assess what's working, for whom, and what could work better.

Community Research

Leading feedback sessions with people currently navigating county systems, keeping findings grounded in what the community is experiencing right now.

A Standing Program

The first year builds the consultant. The program builds the county's capacity.

LELI is not a cohort that ends. It's standing county infrastructure:

A growing roster

Each preparation cycle adds consultants; the county's advisory capacity compounds rather than resets.

Ongoing advisory relationships

Prepared consultants continue working with departments beyond their first year — carrying institutional knowledge across policy cycles, budget years, and plan updates.

Continuous recruitment

As the program matures, recruitment becomes rolling, and experienced consultants help prepare the next generation.

Institutionalized engagement

Meaningful engagement stops being a per-project procurement and becomes a permanent county capacity.

Why It Pays

One investment. Compounding returns.

  • ✓Every engagement hour counts. Preparation happens before advisory work begins, so departments engage consultants who are ready on day one.
  • ✓Findings arrive actionable. System-fluent consultants deliver recommendations mapped to the levers departments control, on the timelines decisions actually run on.
  • ✓Value crosses departments. One consultant's preparation returns value in every system they've navigated — and co-funding distributes the cost.
  • ✓Knowledge compounds. A standing roster retains relationships, context, and institutional memory that grow more valuable each cycle.
