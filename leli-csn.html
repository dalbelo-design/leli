The Problem We Solve

Community input has always lived in silos.

A comment at a Rodeo town hall. A concern raised at a Crockett planning meeting. Testimony delivered in Pacheco. For decades, these signals have existed in isolation — each one heard, briefly, in its own room, by its own set of decision-makers, on its own timeline.

Even when Rodeo, Crockett, Pacheco, Vine Hill, and Clyde were describing the same underlying problem — the same housing pressure, the same transportation gap, the same environmental concern — there was no infrastructure to connect those signals. No way to show a County supervisor that what felt like a local complaint was actually a regional pattern.

The Civic Signals Network is the infrastructure that changes that. It doesn't replace community voice — it amplifies it. It doesn't summarize what people said — it equips Fellows to show what it means.

How It Works

Four steps from community input
to policy action.

The Civic Signals Network is not an AI tool that replaces community judgment. It is a research workspace that Fellows direct — giving them the analytical reach to read thousands of voices without losing sight of any single one.

01

Community Input Is Gathered

Public comments, town hall testimony, survey responses, planning meeting records, and direct community feedback are collected across all five neighborhoods. Input arrives in multiple formats and multiple languages.

Sources include: county public comment systems, city council records, community organization input, direct feedback sessions led by Fellows, and multilingual surveys designed for each neighborhood.
02

AI Processes the Scale

AI handles what no person could: reading thousands of comments at once, translating across languages, clustering similar concerns, and flagging patterns that appear across multiple neighborhoods simultaneously.

What AI does: translation, clustering, pattern flagging, volume summarization, cross-neighborhood comparison.

What AI does not do: interpret meaning, determine significance, make conclusions, or decide what to bring to policymakers. That stays with Fellows.
03

Fellows Analyze and Interpret

Fellows review what the AI surfaced. They verify patterns against their own community knowledge, decide which signals are most significant, document their reasoning, and determine what the findings actually mean for the communities they represent.

Why this matters: an AI can flag that 47 comments mention "the intersection at San Pablo." Only a Fellow who grew up in that neighborhood knows that the reason people keep mentioning it is because a child was hit by a car there two years ago and nothing was done. That context is the difference between data and insight.
04

Findings Reach Decision-Makers

Fellow-produced findings are submitted to active policy processes — City Councils, County hearings, State legislative visits — on timelines that match when decisions are actually being made. Findings are attributed to Fellows and delivered as documented community research, not anecdote.

Formats include: written policy submissions, public testimony with supporting research, cross-neighborhood findings reports shared with residents, and plain-language community briefs in multiple languages.
The Core Design Principle

AI handles scale.
Fellows handle truth.

What AI Does

The machine handles volume.

  • Translates input across languages in real time
  • Reads thousands of public comments simultaneously
  • Clusters similar concerns across neighborhoods
  • Flags patterns that span multiple communities
  • Surfaces what might otherwise be missed by volume alone
  • Summarizes for Fellows to verify — never for publication
What Fellows Do

The community handles truth.

  • Verify AI-surfaced patterns against lived community knowledge
  • Determine which signals are most significant — and why
  • Provide the context that turns data into insight
  • Document reasoning so findings carry weight, not just numbers
  • Decide what to bring forward to policymakers
  • Present and defend findings in civic processes

"Most 'AI-enabled' community engagement quietly replaces community voice with algorithmic summary. The Civic Signals Network is designed to make that failure mode impossible."

Design Principles

Built around three non-negotiables.

The Civic Signals Network was designed by the Civic UX Research Lead with these principles as hard constraints — not aspirations.

01

Transparent reasoning

Every finding produced through the Network is documented with the reasoning behind it — who said what, how it was interpreted, and why it was prioritized. Findings carry weight because the community can see how they were made.

02

Fellow-centered by design

The research workspace is built around Fellow judgment, not algorithmic efficiency. Usability testing with Fellows — conducted by the Civic UX Research Lead — ensures the platform multiplies what Fellows can attend to without obscuring their view of the community.

03

Inclusive by default

AI translation ensures that community input in every language spoken across the five neighborhoods is included in Fellow analysis — not filtered out before it reaches a policymaker. Multi-lingual output means findings can be shared back with the communities that generated them.

What the Network Produces

Findings that carry weight.

The Civic Signals Network doesn't produce algorithmic summaries. It produces community-validated research findings — documented, attributed, and ready for the rooms where decisions are made.

Cross-neighborhood findings reports

Published at the close of each research sprint, these reports surface concerns that span multiple communities — showing County and City decision-makers the full geographic scope of an issue for the first time.

Policy submissions

Formal written submissions to City Councils, County hearings, and State legislative processes — delivered on the timelines those processes require, not after decisions are already made.

Plain-language community briefs

Findings translated into plain language and multiple languages for sharing back with residents, mutual aid networks, faith communities, and local organizations — so the community sees what Fellows found, not just policymakers.

A durable shared-intelligence asset

The Network and its findings don't disappear when the cohort ends. They become a community asset — a growing body of civic research that future Fellows, residents, and organizations can build on.
