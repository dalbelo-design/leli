The LELI Fellowship is open to young adults ages 18–26 living in Rodeo, Crockett, Pacheco, Vine Hill, or Clyde. No prior policy or research experience required — that's what the fellowship builds.

How to Apply

Four simple steps.

The application is designed to be accessible — no writing samples, no resumé required. We want to hear from you in your own words.

01

Fill out the form

Basic info, your neighborhood, and a few short questions about yourself and what draws you to this work. Takes about 20–30 minutes.

02

Introductory conversation

Selected applicants are invited to a brief 30-minute conversation with the LELI team — a chance to ask questions and tell us more about yourself.

03

Cohort orientation

Fellows selected for the cohort attend an orientation session before the fellowship begins in August 2026. We'll cover expectations, logistics, and introductions.

04

Fellowship begins

August 2026. You're a LELI Fellow. Paid, trained, and connected to the civic infrastructure of Contra Costa County.

Frequently Asked Questions

Questions we hear often.

No. The fellowship is designed to build those skills — not to require them upfront. What matters is that you live in or have deep ties to one of the five communities, that you're between 18 and 26, and that you can commit to the full 12 months. Your lived experience of navigating these communities is the foundation everything else is built on.
Yes. Fellows are compensated for their work at rates that reflect the professional value of civic research and lived expertise. The fellowship is not a volunteer opportunity or a stipend-based internship — it's paid work. Specific compensation details will be shared with applicants during the introductory conversation stage.
You may not currently live in Rodeo, Crockett, Pacheco, Vine Hill, or Clyde — but if you grew up there, have family there, work there, or have long-standing community ties there, we want to hear from you. The fellowship is designed for people who understand these communities from the inside, not just people who currently hold a mailing address there.
The fellowship runs August 2026 through July 2027 — 12 months. The specific weekly hours will vary by phase: Phase One (months 1–4) involves more scheduled training and cohort sessions, while Phase Two (months 4–12) is structured around research sprint timelines. We'll share detailed scheduling expectations with applicants before they commit. If you have concerns about scheduling conflicts — work, school, childcare — let us know in your application. Wraparound support is part of what we offer.
Fellow-produced research findings are submitted to real policy and civic decision-making processes — City Councils, County hearings, State legislative visits. Findings are attributed to Fellows, not to the organizations supporting them. Each Fellow will leave with a documented portfolio of civic research work they can point to in future job applications, academic programs, or community leadership roles.
Absolutely. We actively encourage multilingual applicants. The Civic Signals Network includes AI-powered language translation, and we're committed to making the fellowship accessible in every language spoken across the five communities. If you'd like to complete this application or the interview conversation in Spanish or another language, contact us and we'll make it happen.
We review applications on a rolling basis and aim to respond within 2 weeks of receiving your submission. If you're selected for an introductory conversation, we'll reach out to schedule a 30-minute call. If you haven't heard from us after 2 weeks, you're welcome to follow up at any time.

Still have questions?

Reach out to the LELI team directly. We're happy to talk through the application, eligibility, or anything else before you apply.
