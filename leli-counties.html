The Opportunity

Your county is already invested in this. LELI multiplies the return.

Counties across the country are building lived experience engagement into planning, implementation, and evaluation — through advisory bodies, consultant contracts, and governance seats. It's the right direction: the people who have navigated your systems hold expertise no other source can provide.

LELI takes that commitment further. When subject matter experts with lived experience are prepared to understand your county's structures, funding streams, governance, and policy calendar, their expertise becomes fully actionable:

  • ✓Insight that lands. System-fluent consultants can connect what they know to the levers your departments actually control — so recommendations arrive ready to act on.
  • ✓Value from day one. Preparation happens before advisory work begins, so every engagement hour goes toward your priorities.
  • ✓Knowledge that compounds. A standing roster means relationships and learning carry forward across contracts, cohorts, and policy cycles.
  • ✓A signal you can steer by. Consultants trained in community research give your county a continuous picture of how services are landing — between annual counts, in time to act.

The County-Scale Advantage

One expert strengthens every system they've navigated.

Lived experience rarely stays in one lane. The subject matter expert who has navigated your homelessness response system has often also navigated behavioral health, disability services, workforce development, reentry, youth services, or aging supports — frequently several at once, for themselves or for family.

A LELI Consultant is prepared to consult across that whole map. One preparation investment returns value across every department whose system that consultant knows from the inside.

That's why LELI is designed for multi-department co-funding: each participating department contributes a share, each gains access to prepared consultants, and the value multiplies while the cost distributes.

How Counties Fund It

Three ways in. One standing program.

A

Pilot

One or two departments launch the program around an active priority — a strategic plan cycle, a major implementation effort, an evaluation year. Smallest footprint, fastest start, designed to expand.

B

Multi-Department Co-Funded

Participating departments share the program's cost and its consultant roster. Each gains prepared consultants for its own planning and evaluation work; the county gains a shared, cross-system advisory capacity.

C

Full County Integration

LELI operates as permanent county infrastructure — a standing roster, continuous recruitment, and county-wide community research. Meaningful engagement becomes part of how the county does business.

Every implementation is scoped to your county's structure, priorities, and scale.

From Decision to Deployment

What launching LELI looks like.

1

Assessment

We map your county's engagement commitments, active policy cycles, and department priorities to identify where prepared consultants deliver the most immediate value.

2

Design

The framework is customized to your governance structure, service systems, and scale. The funding model and participation agreements take shape with the departments involved.

3

Recruit & Prepare

Consultants are recruited from your community and complete structured preparation: county structures and governance, system fluency, policy language and methodology, consulting and research skills, and AI direction.

4

Deploy

Consultants begin advisory work inside your active processes — plan consultation, implementation workgroups, evaluation, and community feedback sessions.

5

Sustain & Scale

The roster grows, new departments join, and community research deepens. The program compounds year over year.
