About LELI

Built by community.
Designed for real change.

The Lived Experience Leadership Institute was founded on a simple conviction: the people closest to a problem hold the deepest expertise for solving it. LELI exists to make that expertise count — in the rooms, at the tables, and in the policy conversations where decisions are actually made.

Our Story

Where LELI comes from.

LELI grew out of the East Contra Costa Mobility LABs initiative, where community-designed work revealed a persistent gap: residents of unincorporated communities like Rodeo, Crockett, Pacheco, Vine Hill, and Clyde had deep knowledge of what wasn't working — but no structured pathway to carry that knowledge into the policy spaces where it could actually change things.

That work catalyzed $4.25 million in Contra Costa County funding for guaranteed income pilots, and demonstrated that when community members are trained and supported to translate lived experience into policy language, the results are both more equitable and more durable than top-down design alone.

LELI is the infrastructure that institutionalizes that lesson. It is a 12-month fellowship that does not extract community voice — it builds community capacity. Fellows don't just provide input. They become the researchers, the analysts, and the advocates.

The Civic Signals Network — the community-owned research platform at the heart of the fellowship — ensures that the findings Fellows produce carry the weight of rigorous, documented community reasoning, not just algorithmic summary. AI handles the scale. Fellows handle the truth.

"Lived experience can tell you what's wrong. Living expertise can help you build it right."

— The LELI Framework

The Distinction

Lived experience is what you know from being there.

Living expertise is what you can do with it — when you have the research skills, the analytical tools, and the civic access to make it count.

Three Equity Commitments

Designed to disrupt the usual hierarchy.

Most civic engagement programs extract community voice. LELI builds community capacity. Three design commitments keep that distinction real — not rhetorical.

Commitment 01

Lived experience is expertise — and it's compensated that way.

Fellows are paid for their work. This disrupts the tradition of asking the most affected communities to donate their knowledge, on top of everything else.

Commitment 02

Fellows direct the AI. Not the other way around.

The Civic Signals Network is designed so AI multiplies what Fellows can attend to — it never replaces their judgment. Community voice is never quietly replaced by algorithmic summary.

Commitment 03

Recruitment prioritizes those most shut out.

Fellows are recruited from young adults who have navigated economic instability, housing insecurity, or structural barriers to civic participation — the residents whose perspectives are most systematically absent.

Organizational Partners

Three layers of infrastructure.

LELI is delivered through a partnership structure designed to combine community-rooted leadership, proven public-sector delivery capacity, and rigorous charitable compliance.

Dalbert Design

Community-Rooted Lead

A Lived Experience Led Organization founded by Loren Dalbert. Contributes the LELI framework, community relationships across all five neighborhoods, and the lived-experience leadership that anchors Fellow recruitment, curriculum design, and the Civic Signals Network methodology.

CivicMakers

Delivery Partner

A strategic consultancy and community of practitioners celebrating 10 years of human-centered design with governments, nonprofits, and mission-driven organizations. Brings institutional capacity across community engagement, facilitation, training design, and operational infrastructure that meets public-funder standards.

Realize Impact

Fiscal Sponsor · 501(c)(3)

A registered 501(c)(3) public charity specializing in fiscal sponsorship of mission-driven projects. Provides the charitable umbrella, financial oversight, grants administration, and reimbursement-grant cash-flow management — removing compliance risk from the County's perspective.

The Partnership

Together, Dalbert Design and CivicMakers operate as Ridiculously Hopeful Futures in California — a bold initiative building structures where community members and policymakers co-imagine and co-design solutions. LELI is its flagship program.

Equity & Inclusion

Who we center — and why it matters.

LELI serves communities with significantly lower educational attainment than Contra Costa County as a whole, and substantial foreign-born populations whose voices are often missing from county and state policy conversations.
