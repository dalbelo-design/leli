What We Do

We refine lived experience
into living expertise.

Nobody knows how to help the homelessness response system work better than the people who've been inside it. LELI trains them to put that knowledge to work — translating firsthand experience into the analysis, consultation, and partnership that strengthens policy.

01

Leadership Identity

Fellows develop deep self-validation, learning to operate with confidence in rooms where their expertise may not yet be recognized — because it always was.

02

System Fluency

Fellows deepen their firsthand knowledge of the Continuum of Care, coordinated entry, County health and housing services, funding streams, and how decisions get made — so they can contribute as informed partners.

03

Directing AI as a Tool

Fellows learn to direct AI to handle scale — analyzing plans, data, and thousands of community inputs at once — while keeping judgment and meaning-making in human hands.

04

Real Policy Engagement

Fellows bring their analysis to the bodies that guide the County's homelessness response — Continuum of Care committees, County boards, and the public processes where the strategic plan takes shape.

The Civic Signals Network

Community-owned research infrastructure, built by Fellows.

The homelessness response system hears from the community every day — at public hearings, in intake conversations, through surveys and outreach. The Civic Signals Network connects those voices into something the county has never had: a closer-to-real-time picture of homelessness between annual Point-in-Time counts. Fellows read across volume, surface patterns, and carry findings into the strategic planning process — a living feedback loop no single team could build alone.

Why Lived Experience

Experience is the expertise
the system is asking for.

The people working in Contra Costa's homelessness response bring dedication, data, and professional skill. Lived experience consultants bring the one perspective no training can provide: what services feel like from the inside, which handoffs work smoothly, and where a small change would make a big difference. And because lived experience rarely stays in one lane, Fellows carry that same insight into the other systems they've navigated — behavioral health, disability, workforce development, reentry, and aging. Together, that partnership produces plans that work better for everyone, across every service the County delivers.

"The strongest plans are written by the people who run the system and the people who've navigated it — working side by side."
