What We Do

We refine lived experience
into living expertise.

Communities know what's broken. LELI trains young adults to do something about it — translating ground-truth knowledge into the research, analysis, and advocacy that moves policy.

01

Leadership Identity

Fellows develop deep self-validation, learning to operate with confidence in rooms where their expertise may not yet be recognized — because it always was.

02

Civic Intelligence Research

Fellows translate personal experience and community input into system-level insight, analyzing public comments and surfacing patterns across thousands of voices.

03

Directing AI as a Tool

Fellows learn to direct AI to handle scale — translating across languages, processing thousands of comments at once — while keeping judgment and meaning-making in community hands.

04

Real Policy Engagement

Fellows apply those insights at City Councils, County meetings, State hearings, and legislative visits — at the speed and scale policymaking actually requires.

The Fellowship

12 months. Real work.
Lasting impact.

The fellowship runs October 2026 through September 2027. Fellows are paid for their expertise and engaged in real civic processes from day one.

Phase One
Months 1–4

Training in civic intelligence research, the LELI method, and how to direct AI as a research tool. Fellows begin engagement with local government partners and learn to move community input into actionable insight.

The Civic Signals Network

Community-owned research infrastructure, built by Fellows.

Until now, community input has lived in silos — a comment at a Rodeo town hall, a concern at a Crockett planning meeting, a testimony in Pacheco. The Civic Signals Network breaks those silos. Fellows read across volume, surface patterns across neighborhoods, and carry findings into policy conversations with the weight of community-validated insight.

AI handles scale. Fellows handle truth.
